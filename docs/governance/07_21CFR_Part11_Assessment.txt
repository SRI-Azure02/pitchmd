PURPOSE
-------
This document assesses whether PitchMD(tm) constitutes a system subject to the
requirements of 21 CFR Part 11, which governs electronic records and electronic
signatures used in connection with FDA-regulated activities.


REGULATORY BACKGROUND
---------------------
21 CFR Part 11 applies when electronic records are created, modified,
maintained, archived, retrieved, or transmitted UNDER ANY FDA REGULATION THAT
REQUIRES RECORDS.

Key elements required by Part 11 for covered systems:
  - System validation to ensure accuracy, reliability, and consistent operation
  - Audit trails that are computer-generated, date/time-stamped, and secure
  - Access controls limiting system use to authorized individuals
  - Unique user identification and accountability
  - Electronic signatures that are legally binding equivalents of handwritten
    signatures
  - Controls to prevent unauthorized use of electronic signatures
  - Tamper-evident records

CURRENT VERDICT: CONDITIONAL
-----------------------------
21 CFR Part 11 applies to PitchMD(tm) IF the platform is used to satisfy
formally required sales force training documentation obligations.

Even if formal Part 11 obligations do not apply at POC stage, SRI RECOMMENDS
operating as if they do — collecting all data with tamper-evident logs,
server-side timestamps, and user attribution. This ensures no retroactive
data remediation is required at enterprise deployment.

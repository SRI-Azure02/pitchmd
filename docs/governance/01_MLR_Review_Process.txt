PURPOSE
-------
No AI-generated claim, training scenario, or reference material enters the
PitchMD(tm) platform without formal MLR sign-off. This document defines the
end-to-end approval workflow, escalation paths, and emergency update procedures
that govern all platform content for VENCLEXTA (venetoclax) training.

Given VENCLEXTA's Boxed Warning for Tumor Lysis Syndrome (TLS), any content
that relates to dosing, initiation, or TLS management requires particular
scrutiny during the medical and regulatory review stages.
NOTE [Confirmed from PI]: The VENCLEXTA PI Boxed Warning covers Tumor Lysis
Syndrome only. Verbatim Boxed Warning text from the current PI (05/2026):
"Tumor lysis syndrome (TLS), including fatal events and renal failure requiring
dialysis, has occurred in patients treated with VENCLEXTA." The full Boxed
Warning directs prescribers to assess all patients for TLS risk, provide
prophylactic hydration and anti-hyperuricemics, and employ more intensive
measures as risk increases. Embryo-fetal toxicity is covered under Section 5.5
(Warnings and Precautions), NOT as a Boxed Warning.


STANDARD REVIEW WORKFLOW
------------------------
All stages must be completed sequentially. No stage may be skipped without
written approval from the Chief Compliance Officer.

  Stage  Owner                SLA        Activity
  -----  -------------------  ---------  -----------------------------------------
  1      Medical Affairs      Day 0      Content Submission: MA submits proposed
                                          claims, product descriptions, indications,
                                          and training scenarios. For VENCLEXTA, all
                                          TLS-related content flagged for enhanced
                                          medical review in Stage 2.

  2      Medical Affairs      Days 1-3   Medical Review: Reviewer verifies clinical
                                          accuracy against current VENCLEXTA PI,
                                          MURANO/CLL14/AMPLIFY/VIALE-A publications.
                                          Confirms label alignment for CLL/SLL and
                                          AML indications. Special attention to TLS
                                          Boxed Warning accuracy and ramp-up dosing.
                                          NOTE [Confirmed from PI]: AMPLIFY regimen
                                          (VEN+acalabrutinib) is now approved and
                                          in the VENCLEXTA label as of Feb 2026.
                                          Confirm any AMPLIFY training content
                                          matches Section 14 AMPLIFY data exactly.

  3      Legal                Days 3-5   Legal Review: Reviews for liability,
                                          IP, and promotional compliance. Confirms
                                          competitive claims (vs. Imbruvica, Brukinsa,
                                          Calquence, etc.) cite only approved labeling
                                          data. Validates differentiation claims.
                                          NOTE [Confirmed from PI]: IMBRUVICA has
                                          NO Boxed Warning; any training that states
                                          "IMBRUVICA has 4 Boxed Warnings" must be
                                          corrected — IMBRUVICA's safety profile
                                          is addressed via Section 5 W&P only.

  4      Regulatory Affairs   Days 5-7   Regulatory Review: Validates all claims
                                          against the current FDA-approved VENCLEXTA
                                          Prescribing Information (PI) and companion
                                          product PIs (Gazyva, Rituxan, Calquence).
                                          Confirms fair balance requirements are met.
                                          Checks AML indication population restriction
                                          (adults >=75 or comorbid) is accurately
                                          represented.

  5      Compliance           Days 7-8   Compliance Sign-Off: Compliance officer
                                          provides final approval. Content assigned
                                          a unique MLR reference number and entered
                                          into the Approved Claims Library (Doc 2).

  6      IT / Platform Team   Days 8-9   Platform Upload: Approved content uploaded
                                          to the RAG document repository with MLR
                                          reference number, effective date, and
                                          expiry date. TLS/safety content tagged
                                          with FAIR_BALANCE_REQUIRED flag.

  7      Medical Affairs      Ongoing    Periodic Re-Review: All content reviewed
                                          upon VENCLEXTA PI update or annually,
                                          whichever comes first.

ESCALATION PATH
---------------
- Any MLR reviewer may escalate a dispute or borderline content decision
  to the Chief Medical Officer (CMO).
- For TLS-related content disputes: automatic escalation to Chief Medical
  Officer (given the Boxed Warning's significance for patient safety).
- Escalation must be documented in writing. CMO decision within 48 hours.


EMERGENCY CONTENT REMOVAL
--------------------------
If the FDA issues a Drug Safety Communication or updates the VENCLEXTA
Boxed Warning for TLS (or any other safety matter):

  1. Platform Team removes ALL affected content within 24 hours.
  2. Sessions using affected content are flagged in SYNTHETIC_COMPLIANCE_LOG.
  3. Affected content may NOT be re-uploaded until full MLR re-review completes.
  4. Compliance Officer notifies all active PitchMD(tm) users within 24 hours.
  5. If TLS Boxed Warning language is updated: all training scripts, the
     Fair Balance Trigger List (Document 5), and the Approved Claims Library
     (Document 2) must be reviewed before re-activation.

PURPOSE
-------
No AI-generated claim, training scenario, or reference material enters the
PitchMD(tm) platform without formal MLR sign-off. This document defines the
end-to-end approval workflow, escalation paths, and emergency update procedures
that govern all platform content.


STANDARD REVIEW WORKFLOW
------------------------
All stages must be completed sequentially. No stage may be skipped without
written approval from the Chief Compliance Officer.

  Stage  Owner                SLA        Activity
  -----  -------------------  ---------  ----------------------------------------
  1      Medical Affairs      Day 0      Content Submission: MA submits proposed
                                         claims, product descriptions, indications,
                                         and training scenarios for review.

  2      Medical Affairs      Days 1-3   Medical Review: Reviewer verifies clinical
                                         accuracy, label alignment, and appropriate
                                         citation of supporting evidence.

  3      Legal                Days 3-5   Legal Review: Counsel reviews for liability
                                         exposure, IP considerations, and
                                         promotional compliance.

  4      Regulatory Affairs   Days 5-7   Regulatory Review: Validates all claims
                                         against current FDA-approved Prescribing
                                         Information (PI).

  5      Compliance           Days 7-8   Compliance Sign-Off: Compliance officer
                                         provides final approval. Content is assigned
                                         a unique MLR reference number.

  6      IT / Platform Team   Days 8-9   Platform Upload: Approved content uploaded
                                         to the RAG document repository with MLR
                                         reference number, effective date, and
                                         expiry date.

  7      Medical Affairs      Ongoing    Periodic Re-Review: All content reviewed
                                         upon label update or annually, whichever
                                         comes first.


ESCALATION PATH
---------------
- Any MLR reviewer may escalate a dispute or borderline content decision to
  the Chief Medical Officer (CMO).
- Escalation must be documented in writing with: the specific concern, proposed
  resolution, and final CMO decision.
- Escalation does not pause the SLA clock. CMO decision must be received within
  48 hours of escalation being raised.


EMERGENCY CONTENT REMOVAL
--------------------------
If the FDA issues a Drug Safety Communication, MedWatch alert, or any safety
labeling update affecting a product represented in PitchMD(tm):

  1. The Platform Team must remove all affected content within 24 hours of
     the FDA communication.
  2. Sessions that used affected content must be flagged in the Compliance
     Audit Log.
  3. Affected content may NOT be re-uploaded until a full MLR re-review is
     completed per the standard workflow above.
  4. The Compliance Officer must notify all active PitchMD(tm) users of the
     content removal within 24 hours.


DOCUMENT RETENTION
------------------
All MLR review records — including submission packages, reviewer comments,
approval certificates, and re-review documentation — must be retained for a
minimum of SEVEN (7) YEARS, consistent with FDA record retention requirements
under 21 CFR Part 202.1(j).

Records must be stored in a tamper-evident, auditable document management system.

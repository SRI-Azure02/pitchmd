PURPOSE
-------
The Operational Design Domain (ODD) defines the universe of topics that
PitchMD(tm) is permitted to address during training sessions for VENCLEXTA
(venetoclax) sales representatives.

Physician personas in PitchMD(tm) will be trained as hematology/oncology
specialists (hematologists, medical oncologists) who manage patients with
CLL/SLL and/or AML. The ODD reflects what is appropriate for a Venclexta
rep to discuss during a physician office call or hospital visit.


SECTION 6.1 — IN-SCOPE TOPICS (PERMITTED)
------------------------------------------

  CLINICAL TOPICS (VENCLEXTA-SPECIFIC)

  - CLL/SLL approved indications:
      Frontline CLL/SLL + obinutuzumab (CLL14)
      Frontline CLL/SLL + acalabrutinib (AMPLIFY, Feb 2026)
      Relapsed/refractory CLL/SLL + rituximab (MURANO)
      Relapsed/refractory CLL/SLL monotherapy

  - AML approved indication:
      Newly diagnosed AML (adults >=75 or unfit for intensive chemo) with
      azacitidine, decitabine, or LDAC (VIALE-A, VIALE-C)

  - BCL-2 mechanism of action (as described in PI)

  - Pivotal trial data explicitly cited in approved labeling:
      MURANO, CLL14, AMPLIFY, VIALE-A, VIALE-C, M14-032

  - TLS Boxed Warning and management (5-week dose ramp-up, prophylaxis)

  - Full safety profile from the PI:
      Neutropenia, infections, tumor lysis syndrome, thrombocytopenia

  - Dosing and administration as specified in the PI:
      5-week ramp-up for CLL/SLL
      Standard daily dosing for AML combinations

  - Drug interactions per PI (CYP3A inhibitors, P-gp inhibitors)

  - Patient support programs (VENCLEXTA Access Solutions hub services,
    co-pay assistance for eligible patients)

  - Formulary and payer access (factual status; no coverage guarantees)

  - Handling of common objections using data from the Approved Claims Library:
      Fixed-duration vs. continuous therapy discussion
      TLS management and prophylaxis protocols
      del(17p) and high-risk CLL patient data
      Sequencing after BTK inhibitor failure

  - Competitor product safety/efficacy discussion ONLY using data
    from the Approved Claims Library and competitor PIs (factual only):
      Ibrutinib Boxed Warnings for cardiac/bleeding risk
      ALPINE trial data on zanubrutinib vs. ibrutinib
      Pirtobrutinib positioning as post-BTK AND post-venetoclax option


  PHYSICIAN CONTEXT TOPICS

  - Standard of care for CLL/SLL (frontline and relapsed/refractory)
  - Standard of care for unfit/elderly AML patients
  - CLL staging (Rai/Binet), risk stratification (del(17p), TP53, IGHV)
  - MRD (minimal residual disease) as an emerging endpoint
  - Treatment sequencing and therapy selection discussions
  - AML comorbidity assessment (excluding intensive chemotherapy)
  - Physician prescribing patterns and formulary access in their institution

SECTION 6.3 — PHYSICIAN PERSONA CONFIGURATION
----------------------------------------------
Physician personas in PitchMD(tm) will be configured as:

  Primary specialty: Hematology / Hematologic Oncology
  Secondary specialty: Medical Oncology (may prescribe venetoclax for AML)

  Typical patient volume (simulation context):
    - CLL/SLL patients: High (hematology-focused)
    - AML patients: Moderate to High (depending on practice setting)
    - Prescribed BTK inhibitors: Yes (will have context on ibrutinib,
      zanubrutinib, acalabrutinib)
    - Venetoclax experience: Varies by persona (treatment-naive to experienced)

  Personas will NOT be configured as:
    - Primary care physicians (outside scope)
    - Oncologists managing only solid tumors
    - Pharmacists or nurses (training is for physician interaction)


SECTION 6.4 — REPEATED VIOLATION ESCALATION
--------------------------------------------
Single ODD violation:
  - Compliance flag in SYNTHETIC_COMPLIANCE_LOG
  - User sees redirect message
  - No immediate account action

Three or more violations of the same category within rolling 30 days:
  - Escalation notification to rep's direct manager
  - Escalation notification to Compliance Officer
  - In-app notice to user

Multiple Myeloma off-label discussion (any frequency):
  - Immediate flag regardless of frequency
  - Requires manager review before session continuation
  (Rationale: VEN + bortezomib/dex increased mortality signal makes this
   a high-severity off-label risk)

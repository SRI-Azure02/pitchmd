PURPOSE
-------
The Operational Design Domain (ODD) defines the universe of topics that
PitchMD(tm) is permitted to address during training sessions. It functions as
the "topic guardrail" — enforced by the Phase 3 Input Firewall — that keeps
all training interactions focused on compliant pharmaceutical promotion.

Any user input or AI-generated output that falls outside this domain will be
blocked, redirected, or flagged according to the action specified below.


SECTION 6.1 — IN-SCOPE TOPICS (PERMITTED)
------------------------------------------
The following topics are within the permitted operational domain of PitchMD(tm).
The AI physician persona may discuss any of these topics, and sales
representative inputs on these topics will be processed normally.

  TOPIC AREA                    SCOPE BOUNDARY
  ----------------------------  -----------------------------------------------
  Approved Product Indications  As listed in the Approved Claims Library only
                                 (Document 2). No extrapolation permitted.

  Mechanism of Action           As described in the current FDA-approved PI.
                                 No speculative MoA discussion permitted.

  Clinical Trial Data           Only trials explicitly cited in approved labeling.
                                 Trial data from peer-reviewed publications not
                                 in the PI requires MLR review before use.

  Dosing and Administration     Per PI only. No alternative dosing regimens.

  Patient Support Programs      Factual descriptions of co-pay assistance, hub
                                 services, and patient access programs.

  Formulary and Payer Access    Factual, non-speculative. Current formulary
                                 status only; no future coverage promises.

  Objection Handling            Using approved clinical data and communication
                                 techniques only. No prohibited language (Doc 3).

  Adverse Event Profiles        Approved safety data from PI and REMS materials
                                 only. Must be accompanied by fair balance
                                 (see Document 5).

  Comparative Claims            ONLY if head-to-head data is in the approved
                                 labeling AND listed in the Claims Library.

SECTION 6.3 — REPEATED VIOLATION ESCALATION
--------------------------------------------
Single violation:
  - Generates a compliance flag in SYNTHETIC_COMPLIANCE_LOG.
  - User sees the redirect message.
  - No immediate user notification beyond the redirect.

Repeated violations (3+ of same category within rolling 30 days):
  - System generates an escalation notification to:
      (a) The user's direct manager
      (b) The Compliance Officer
  - User receives an in-app notice that their account is under review.

Severe violations (PHI disclosure, jailbreak attempts):
  - Immediate flag to Compliance Officer regardless of frequency.
  - Session may be suspended pending compliance review at Compliance
    Officer's discretion.

PURPOSE
-------
The Operational Design Domain (ODD) defines the universe of topics that
PitchMD(tm) is permitted to address during training sessions for VENCLEXTA
(venetoclax) sales representatives.

Physician personas in PitchMD(tm) will be trained as hematology/oncology
specialists (hematologists, medical oncologists) who manage patients with
CLL/SLL and/or AML. The ODD reflects what is appropriate for a Venclexta
rep to discuss during a physician office call or hospital visit.


SECTION 6.1 — IN-SCOPE TOPICS (PERMITTED)
------------------------------------------

  CLINICAL TOPICS (VENCLEXTA-SPECIFIC)

  - CLL/SLL approved indications:
      Frontline CLL/SLL + obinutuzumab (CLL14)
      Frontline CLL/SLL + acalabrutinib (AMPLIFY, Feb 2026)
      Relapsed/refractory CLL/SLL + rituximab (MURANO)
      Relapsed/refractory CLL/SLL monotherapy
      [Confirmed from PI, Section 1.1 and 2.2]: The CLL/SLL indication text
      is broad (does not specify frontline vs. R/R). Specific regimens are
      addressed in Section 2.2. The AMPLIFY regimen approved in the PI is the
      2-agent VEN+acalabrutinib combination (without obinutuzumab). The
      VEN+ACA+obinutuzumab (AVO) arm is described in Section 5.3 as an
      "unapproved regimen" — training must NOT include the triple AVO arm as
      an approved indication. The AMPLIFY enrollment criterion (patients without
      del(17p) or TP53 mutation) is an important patient selection note.

  - AML approved indication:
      Newly diagnosed AML (adults >=75 or with comorbidities that preclude
      intensive induction chemotherapy) with azacitidine, decitabine, or
      low-dose cytarabine (VIALE-A, VIALE-C)
      [Confirmed from PI, Section 1.2]: The exact PI phrase is "adults 75
      years or older, or who have comorbidities that preclude use of intensive
      induction chemotherapy." Training must not substitute "unfit" for this
      language without citing the PI qualifier. The PI uses the term "low-dose
      cytarabine" in the indication text (not the abbreviation "LDAC").

  - BCL-2 mechanism of action (as described in PI)

  - Pivotal trial data explicitly cited in approved labeling:
      MURANO, CLL14, AMPLIFY, VIALE-A, VIALE-C, M14-032, M13-982
      [Confirmed from PI, Section 14]: All trial names listed are confirmed
      in Section 14 of the current VENCLEXTA PI (revised 05/2026). M14-032
      (post-BTK/post-idelalisib monotherapy) and M13-982 (del(17p) monotherapy)
      are both confirmed in Section 14.1. VIALE-C data is included in Section
      14.2; the PI confirms the primary OS endpoint was NOT met (HR 0.75;
      p=0.114). Training must disclose the failed primary endpoint when citing
      VIALE-C data.

  - TLS Boxed Warning and management (5-week dose ramp-up, prophylaxis)
      [Confirmed from PI, Section 2.2 and 5.1]: The 5-week CLL/SLL dose
      ramp-up (20 mg → 50 mg → 100 mg → 200 mg → 400 mg weekly) is confirmed
      in Section 2.2 of the current VENCLEXTA PI (revised 05/2026). The PI
      Section 5.1 provides TLS risk stratification by tumor burden (lymph node
      size and ALC) to guide prophylaxis intensity and monitoring. High-risk
      patients may require hospitalization for initial doses — this risk-
      stratified management approach should be reflected in training content.

  - Full safety profile from the PI:
      Neutropenia, infections, tumor lysis syndrome, thrombocytopenia,
      diarrhea, nausea, upper respiratory tract infection, fatigue,
      musculoskeletal pain, edema, cough, anemia, pyrexia, vomiting
      [Confirmed from PI, Section 5 and 6.1]: Section 5 Warnings and
      Precautions confirmed: 5.1 TLS (Boxed Warning); 5.2 Neutropenia;
      5.3 Infections; 5.4 Hepatotoxicity; 5.5 Embryo-Fetal Toxicity;
      5.6 Multiple Myeloma (increased mortality, discouraged outside trials).
      Section 6.1 AE tables confirm diarrhea (43%), nausea (42%), URTI (36%),
      fatigue (32%), anemia (33%), thrombocytopenia (29%), musculoskeletal
      pain (29%), edema (22%), cough (22%) from monotherapy CLL/SLL data.

  - Dosing and administration as specified in the PI:
      5-week ramp-up for CLL/SLL; AML-specific ramp-up schedules
      [Confirmed from PI, Section 2.2 and 2.3]:
      (a) CLL/SLL ramp-up: 20→50→100→200→400 mg weekly (5 weeks) — confirmed
      (b) CLL/SLL maintenance: 400 mg once daily after ramp-up — confirmed
      (c) AML dosing with AZA or decitabine: 3-day ramp-up (100/200/400 mg
          Days 1/2/3+), then 400 mg once daily continuously — confirmed
      (d) AML dosing with low-dose cytarabine: 4-day ramp-up (100/200/400/600
          mg Days 1/2/3/4+), then 600 mg once daily — confirmed
      (e) Dose modifications for cytopenias, infections, drug interactions
          are specified in Section 2.5–2.6 of the current PI — confirmed
      All training must reflect current Section 2 language exactly.

  - Drug interactions per PI (CYP3A inhibitors, P-gp inhibitors)

  - Patient support programs (VENCLEXTA Access Solutions hub services,
    co-pay assistance for eligible patients)

  - Formulary and payer access (factual status; no coverage guarantees)

  - Handling of common objections using data from the Approved Claims Library:
      Fixed-duration vs. continuous therapy discussion
      TLS management and prophylaxis protocols
      del(17p) and high-risk CLL patient data
      Sequencing after BTK inhibitor failure

  - Competitor product safety/efficacy discussion ONLY using data
    from the Approved Claims Library and competitor PIs (factual only):
      Ibrutinib Section 5 Warnings for cardiac/bleeding risk
        [Confirmed from PI, IMBRUVICA PI revised 10/2025]: IMBRUVICA has
        ZERO Boxed Warnings. All safety items (Hemorrhage, Infections,
        Cardiac Arrhythmias/Cardiac Failure/Sudden Death, Hypertension,
        Cytopenias, Second Primary Malignancies, Hepatotoxicity, TLS, Embryo-
        Fetal Toxicity) are Section 5 Warnings and Precautions. Training must
        not state "IMBRUVICA has 4 Boxed Warnings" — this is incorrect.
      ALPINE trial data on zanubrutinib vs. ibrutinib
        [Confirmed from PI, BRUKINSA PI revised 06/2025]: BRUKINSA has ZERO
        Boxed Warnings. SEQUOIA data confirmed in Section 14.4. Medical Affairs
        to confirm whether specific ALPINE HR 0.68 and AF rate data appear in
        the current BRUKINSA PI Section 14 before permitting in training.
      Pirtobrutinib positioning as post-BTK AND post-venetoclax option
        [Confirmed from PI, JAYPIRCA PI]: JAYPIRCA has ZERO Boxed Warnings.
        CLL/SLL indication is confirmed: after prior covalent BTK inhibitor
        (full approval). The indication text requires prior BTK inhibitor but
        does NOT formally require prior venetoclax/BCL-2 inhibitor in its
        verbatim language (though the BRUIN study population had both).
      COPIKTRA Boxed Warning for Treatment-Related Mortality
        [Confirmed from PI, COPIKTRA PI]: COPIKTRA has 5 Boxed Warnings.
        First Boxed Warning is Treatment-Related Mortality (15% of patients),
        not an HR vs. ofatumumab. Do not cite HR 1.09 as a Boxed Warning item.
        LIMITATIONS OF USE [Confirmed from PI, Section 1]: COPIKTRA is not
        indicated or recommended for initial or second line CLL/SLL treatment
        due to increased risk of treatment-related mortality.
      ZYDELIG Boxed Warnings (5 categories — corrected from prior count of 4)
        [Confirmed from PI, ZYDELIG PI]: ZYDELIG has 5 Boxed Warning items:
        (1) Hepatotoxicity (16%); (2) Severe diarrhea/colitis (20%);
        (3) Pneumonitis (4%); (4) Infections (48%); (5) Intestinal Perforation.
        Boxed Warning heading: "FATAL AND SERIOUS TOXICITIES: HEPATIC, SEVERE
        DIARRHEA, COLITIS, PNEUMONITIS, INFECTIONS, and INTESTINAL PERFORATION."
        CONTRAINDICATION [Confirmed from PI, Section 4]: History of serious
        hypersensitivity to idelalisib (including anaphylaxis) or toxic
        epidermal necrolysis with any drug.


  PHYSICIAN CONTEXT TOPICS

  - Standard of care for CLL/SLL (frontline and relapsed/refractory)
  - Standard of care for unfit/elderly AML patients
  - CLL staging (Rai/Binet), risk stratification (del(17p), TP53, IGHV)
  - MRD (minimal residual disease) as an emerging endpoint
  - Treatment sequencing and therapy selection discussions
  - AML comorbidity assessment (excluding intensive chemotherapy)
  - Physician prescribing patterns and formulary access in their institution

SECTION 6.3 — PHYSICIAN PERSONA CONFIGURATION
----------------------------------------------
Physician personas in PitchMD(tm) will be configured as:

  Primary specialty: Hematology / Hematologic Oncology
  Secondary specialty: Medical Oncology (may prescribe venetoclax for AML)

  Typical patient volume (simulation context):
    - CLL/SLL patients: High (hematology-focused)
    - AML patients: Moderate to High (depending on practice setting)
    - Prescribed BTK inhibitors: Yes (will have context on ibrutinib,
      zanubrutinib, acalabrutinib)
    - Venetoclax experience: Varies by persona (treatment-naive to experienced)

  Personas will NOT be configured as:
    - Primary care physicians (outside scope)
    - Oncologists managing only solid tumors
    - Pharmacists or nurses (training is for physician interaction)


SECTION 6.4 — REPEATED VIOLATION ESCALATION
--------------------------------------------
Single ODD violation:
  - Compliance flag in SYNTHETIC_COMPLIANCE_LOG
  - User sees redirect message
  - No immediate account action

Three or more violations of the same category within rolling 30 days:
  - Escalation notification to rep's direct manager
  - Escalation notification to Compliance Officer
  - In-app notice to user

Multiple Myeloma off-label discussion (any frequency):
  - Immediate flag regardless of frequency
  - Requires manager review before session continuation
  (Rationale: VEN + bortezomib/dex increased mortality signal makes this
   a high-severity off-label risk)

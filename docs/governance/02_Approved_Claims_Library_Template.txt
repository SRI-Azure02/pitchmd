--- APPROVED INDICATIONS ---

VEN-IND-001: CLL/SLL
  "For the treatment of adult patients with chronic lymphocytic leukemia
   (CLL) or small lymphocytic lymphoma (SLL)."
  Sub-categories:
    - Frontline CLL/SLL in combination with obinutuzumab (CLL14)
    - Frontline CLL/SLL in combination with acalabrutinib (AMPLIFY, Feb 2026)
    - Relapsed/refractory CLL/SLL in combination with rituximab (MURANO)
    - Relapsed/refractory CLL/SLL monotherapy

VEN-IND-002: AML
  "In combination with azacitidine, or decitabine, or low-dose cytarabine
   for the treatment of newly diagnosed acute myeloid leukemia (AML) in
   adults 75 years or older, or who have comorbidities that preclude use
   of intensive induction chemotherapy."
  Source: VIALE-A (azacitidine), VIALE-C (LDAC), decitabine data


--- MECHANISM OF ACTION ---

VEN-MOA-001: BCL-2 Inhibition
  "VENCLEXTA is a selective, potent small-molecule inhibitor of BCL-2
   (B-cell lymphoma 2 protein), the first and only approved BCL-2 inhibitor.
   BCL-2 overexpression allows CLL and AML cells to evade programmed cell
   death. VENCLEXTA binds directly to BCL-2 and displaces pro-apoptotic
   proteins (such as BIM), triggering cancer cell death."
  Source: VENCLEXTA PI, Section 12.1 (Mechanism of Action)
  MLR Status: [TO BE COMPLETED]


--- CLL/SLL EFFICACY CLAIMS ---

VEN-EFF-001: MURANO — Primary PFS Endpoint (R/R CLL)
  "In the MURANO trial (relapsed/refractory CLL), VENCLEXTA + rituximab
   demonstrated a median PFS of 57.4 months vs. 17.0 months for
   bendamustine + rituximab (HR 0.23; 95% CI 0.18-0.29) at final analysis."
  Source: MURANO Phase 3 Trial, Final Analysis
  MLR Status: [TO BE COMPLETED]

VEN-EFF-002: MURANO — 5-Year PFS Rate
  "In the MURANO trial, the 5-year PFS rate was 57.3% with VENCLEXTA +
   rituximab versus 4.6% with bendamustine + rituximab."
  Source: MURANO Phase 3 Trial, Final Analysis
  MLR Status: [TO BE COMPLETED]

VEN-EFF-003: MURANO — Overall Survival
  "In the MURANO trial, median OS was not reached with VENCLEXTA + rituximab
   vs. 87.8 months with bendamustine + rituximab (HR 0.53; 95% CI 0.37-0.74).
   7-year OS rates: 69.6% vs. 51%."
  Source: MURANO Phase 3 Trial, Blood (ASH 2025)
  MLR Status: [TO BE COMPLETED]

VEN-EFF-004: MURANO — Fixed Duration
  "VENCLEXTA + rituximab is a fixed-duration, 2-year regimen (not continuous
   daily therapy). Patients complete treatment and may stop therapy."
  Source: MURANO Phase 3 Trial Protocol; VENCLEXTA PI
  MLR Status: [TO BE COMPLETED]

VEN-EFF-005: CLL14 — Primary PFS Endpoint (Treatment-Naive CLL)
  "In the CLL14 trial (treatment-naive CLL with comorbidities), VENCLEXTA +
   obinutuzumab demonstrated a median PFS not reached vs. 36.4 months for
   chlorambucil + obinutuzumab (HR 0.33; 95% CI 0.25-0.45; P<0.0001) at
   52.4-month follow-up."
  Source: CLL14 Phase 3 Trial
  MLR Status: [TO BE COMPLETED]

VEN-EFF-006: CLL14 — 4-Year PFS Rate
  "In the CLL14 trial, the 4-year PFS rate was 74.0% with VENCLEXTA +
   obinutuzumab versus 35.4% with chlorambucil + obinutuzumab."
  Source: CLL14 Phase 3 Trial
  MLR Status: [TO BE COMPLETED]

VEN-EFF-007: CLL14 — ORR
  "In the CLL14 trial, overall response rate was 85% with VENCLEXTA +
   obinutuzumab vs. 71% with chlorambucil + obinutuzumab (P=0.0007)."
  Source: CLL14 Phase 3 Trial
  MLR Status: [TO BE COMPLETED]

VEN-EFF-008: CLL14 — Fixed Duration
  "The VENCLEXTA + obinutuzumab regimen in CLL14 is a fixed 12-month
   treatment duration (not continuous). Treatment concludes after
   approximately 12 months of therapy."
  Source: CLL14 Phase 3 Trial Protocol; VENCLEXTA PI
  MLR Status: [TO BE COMPLETED]

VEN-EFF-009: AMPLIFY — All-Oral Fixed-Duration Frontline CLL (Feb 2026)
  "In the AMPLIFY trial (treatment-naive CLL/SLL), the all-oral fixed-duration
   regimen of VENCLEXTA + acalabrutinib demonstrated a 3-year PFS rate of
   76.5% (without obinutuzumab) and 83.1% (with obinutuzumab), vs. 66.5%
   for standard chemoimmunotherapy (HR 0.65; P=0.0038) — the first all-oral,
   fixed-duration frontline CLL regimen approved."
  Source: AMPLIFY Phase 3 Trial; FDA Approval February 19, 2026
  MLR Status: [TO BE COMPLETED]

VEN-EFF-010: del(17p) Activity
  "VENCLEXTA demonstrated activity in CLL patients with del(17p), a
   high-risk genetic feature associated with poor outcomes with
   chemoimmunotherapy."
  Source: VENCLEXTA PI; CLL14 subgroup analysis; MURANO subgroup analysis
  MLR Status: [TO BE COMPLETED]

--- SAFETY CLAIMS (Required for Fair Balance) ---

VEN-SAF-001: BOXED WARNING — Tumor Lysis Syndrome (CLL/SLL)
  "BOXED WARNING: Tumor lysis syndrome (TLS), including fatal events and
   renal failure requiring dialysis, has occurred in patients treated with
   VENCLEXTA. Assess all patients for TLS risk prior to initiation. Provide
   appropriate prophylaxis including hydration and anti-hyperuricemics.
   Employ more intensive measures for higher-risk patients. Use the 5-week
   dose ramp-up schedule in CLL/SLL patients."
  Source: VENCLEXTA PI, Boxed Warning
  MLR Status: [TO BE COMPLETED]
  NOTE: This claim MUST be paired with any CLL/SLL efficacy claim.

VEN-SAF-002: TLS — Ramp-Up Dosing Schedule
  "VENCLEXTA requires a 5-week dose ramp-up in CLL/SLL patients (20 mg ->
   50 mg -> 100 mg -> 200 mg -> 400 mg) to reduce the risk of TLS. With
   current ramp-up and prophylaxis protocols, TLS occurred in 2% of patients
   in CLL/SLL monotherapy trials (vs. 12% with earlier protocols)."
  Source: VENCLEXTA PI, Sections 2 and 5.1
  MLR Status: [TO BE COMPLETED]

VEN-SAF-003: Neutropenia (CLL/SLL)
  "The most common Grade 3 or 4 adverse reaction in patients with CLL/SLL
   treated with VENCLEXTA was neutropenia (63-64%). Monitor blood counts
   regularly. Grade 3-4 neutropenia may require dose interruptions."
  Source: VENCLEXTA PI, Section 6.1
  MLR Status: [TO BE COMPLETED]

VEN-SAF-004: Most Common Adverse Events — CLL Monotherapy
  "The most common adverse events (any grade, >=20%) in patients with
   CLL/SLL treated with VENCLEXTA monotherapy include: neutropenia (50%),
   diarrhea (43%), nausea (42%), upper respiratory tract infection (36%),
   anemia (33%), fatigue (32%), thrombocytopenia (29%), musculoskeletal pain
   (29%), edema (22%), and cough (22%)."
  Source: VENCLEXTA PI, Section 6.1
  MLR Status: [TO BE COMPLETED]

VEN-SAF-005: CYP3A Inhibitors Contraindication (CLL/SLL)
  "Concomitant use of VENCLEXTA with strong CYP3A inhibitors at initiation
   and during the ramp-up phase in patients with CLL/SLL is CONTRAINDICATED
   due to increased exposure and risk of TLS."
  Source: VENCLEXTA PI, Sections 4 and 7.1
  MLR Status: [TO BE COMPLETED]

VEN-SAF-006: No REMS Program
  "VENCLEXTA does not have a REMS program. TLS risk is managed through the
   dose ramp-up schedule, required prophylaxis measures, and monitoring as
   outlined in the Prescribing Information."
  Source: VENCLEXTA PI; FDA approval history
  MLR Status: [TO BE COMPLETED]

VEN-SAF-007: Most Common Adverse Events — AML (VIALE-A)
  "In VIALE-A, the most frequent Grade 3 or 4 adverse reactions in patients
   treated with VENCLEXTA + azacitidine were thrombocytopenia, neutropenia,
   and febrile neutropenia. Grade 3-4 infections including pneumonia were
   reported in approximately 33% of patients."
  Source: VENCLEXTA PI, Section 6.1 (AML); VIALE-A publication
  MLR Status: [TO BE COMPLETED]

--- IMBRUVICA (ibrutinib) — Covalent BTK Inhibitor, 1st Generation ---

COMP-IBR-001: Approved Indications
  CLL/SLL (frontline and R/R); Waldenström's Macroglobulinemia;
  cGVHD (adults and pediatric ≥1 year)
  NOTE: MCL and MZL indications VOLUNTARILY WITHDRAWN May 2023.

COMP-IBR-002: Boxed Warnings (4 warnings)
  (1) Serious hemorrhage including fatal hemorrhage
  (2) Serious infections including fatal infections
  (3) Cardiac arrhythmias and cardiac failure
  (4) Hypertension

COMP-IBR-003: Atrial Fibrillation Risk
  Atrial fibrillation/flutter reported in approximately 9-17% of patients;
  Grade >=3 AF in 3.7%; increases with duration of therapy.
  Source: IMBRUVICA PI


--- BRUKINSA (zanubrutinib) — Covalent BTK Inhibitor, 2nd Generation ---

COMP-ZAN-001: ALPINE Head-to-Head vs. Ibrutinib (R/R CLL)
  Zanubrutinib demonstrated superior PFS vs. ibrutinib (HR 0.68; 95% CI
  0.54-0.84) and significantly lower atrial fibrillation rates (7.1% vs.
  17.0%) in the ALPINE Phase 3 trial. Source: ALPINE Phase 3 Trial, Blood

COMP-ZAN-002: Approved Indications
  CLL/SLL (frontline and R/R); MCL (≥1 prior therapy); WM; MZL (R/R);
  FL (R/R, in combination with obinutuzumab, ≥2 prior lines)


--- CALQUENCE (acalabrutinib) — Covalent BTK Inhibitor, 2nd Generation ---

COMP-ACA-001: Approved Indications
  CLL/SLL (frontline and R/R); previously untreated MCL (with BR, Jan 2025);
  CLL/SLL (frontline, in combination with venetoclax, Feb 2026 — AMPLIFY)

COMP-ACA-002: Headache as Distinctive AE
  Headache reported in 37-43% of patients treated with acalabrutinib —
  the most distinctive adverse event profile vs. other BTK inhibitors.
  Source: CALQUENCE PI


--- GAZYVA (obinutuzumab) — Type II Anti-CD20 Antibody ---

COMP-GAZ-001: Role as Venetoclax Partner
  Gazyva (obinutuzumab) is the anti-CD20 antibody partner in the CLL14
  fixed-duration regimen (VEN+G). It is also FDA-approved for previously
  untreated and relapsed/refractory follicular lymphoma.
  Source: CLL14 PI; GAZYVA PI

COMP-GAZ-002: Boxed Warnings
  (1) HBV Reactivation (screen all patients before treatment)
  (2) Progressive Multifocal Leukoencephalopathy (PML)
  Infusion-related reactions (IRRs) in approximately 69% of patients
  (mostly Cycle 1, Day 1). Source: GAZYVA PI


--- JAYPIRCA (pirtobrutinib) — Non-Covalent BTK Inhibitor ---

COMP-JAY-001: Unique Positioning — Post-BTK AND Post-Venetoclax
  Jaypirca is FDA-approved for adult patients with CLL/SLL who have received
  at least two prior lines including a BTK inhibitor AND a BCL-2 inhibitor
  (venetoclax) — the only approved option in this late-line post-both-classes
  setting (full approval December 2025).
  Source: JAYPIRCA PI

COMP-JAY-002: Non-Covalent Mechanism — Overcomes C481S Mutation
  Unlike covalent BTK inhibitors (ibrutinib, zanubrutinib, acalabrutinib),
  pirtobrutinib does not form a covalent bond with BTK, allowing it to retain
  activity against the C481S resistance mutation that drives resistance to
  covalent BTK inhibitors. Source: JAYPIRCA PI


--- RITUXAN (rituximab) — Type I Anti-CD20 Antibody ---

COMP-RIT-001: Role as Venetoclax Partner
  Rituximab is the anti-CD20 partner in the MURANO regimen (VEN+R) for
  relapsed/refractory CLL/SLL. Also approved for DLBCL, FL, and other NHL
  indications. Source: RITUXAN PI; MURANO Phase 3 Trial

COMP-RIT-002: Boxed Warnings (4 warnings)
  (1) Fatal infusion-related reactions
  (2) Severe mucocutaneous reactions
  (3) Progressive Multifocal Leukoencephalopathy (PML)
  (4) Hepatitis B Virus (HBV) Reactivation
  Source: RITUXAN PI


--- COPIKTRA (duvelisib) — PI3K-delta/gamma Inhibitor ---

COMP-DUV-001: Approved Indication and Limitations
  Approved for R/R CLL/SLL after >=2 prior therapies. IMPORTANT: 2022 updated
  Boxed Warning added for possible increased risk of death vs. ofatumumab at
  5-year follow-up (HR 1.09). Source: FDA Safety Communication June 2022

COMP-DUV-002: Boxed Warnings (5 warnings — heavily burdened)
  (1) Fatal/serious infections  (2) Fatal/serious diarrhea or colitis
  (3) Fatal/serious cutaneous reactions  (4) Fatal/serious pneumonitis
  (5) Increased risk of death vs. ofatumumab (2022 addition)
  Source: COPIKTRA PI (2022 update)


--- ZYDELIG (idelalisib) — PI3K-delta Inhibitor ---

COMP-ZYD-001: Approved Indication and Context
  Approved for relapsed CLL in combination with rituximab (for patients where
  rituximab alone would be appropriate due to comorbidities, ≥1 prior therapy);
  relapsed FL monotherapy (≥2 prior lines); relapsed SLL monotherapy (≥2 lines).
  Source: ZYDELIG PI

COMP-ZYD-002: Boxed Warnings and Clinical Context
  Boxed Warnings: (1) Fatal/serious hepatotoxicity  (2) Severe diarrhea/colitis
  (3) Fatal/serious pneumonitis  (4) Fatal/serious intestinal perforation.
  NOTE: In 2016, FDA halted 6 idelalisib combination studies due to increased
  adverse events and deaths. Use is limited to narrow approved indications
  with intensive monitoring. Source: ZYDELIG PI; FDA safety communications

--- MECHANISM OF ACTION ---

VEN-MOA-001: BCL-2 Inhibition
  Verbatim from PI Section 12.1 [Confirmed from PI, Section 12.1]:
  "Venetoclax is a selective and orally bioavailable small-molecule inhibitor
  of BCL-2, an anti-apoptotic protein. Overexpression of BCL-2 has been
  demonstrated in CLL and AML cells where it mediates tumor cell survival and
  has been associated with resistance to chemotherapeutics. Venetoclax helps
  restore the process of apoptosis by binding directly to the BCL-2 protein,
  displacing pro-apoptotic proteins like BIM, triggering mitochondrial outer
  membrane permeabilization and the activation of caspases. In nonclinical
  studies, venetoclax has demonstrated cytotoxic activity in tumor cells that
  overexpress BCL-2."
  Source: VENCLEXTA PI, Section 12.1
  MLR Status: [TO BE COMPLETED]
  NOTE: The claim "first and only approved BCL-2 inhibitor" is a promotional
  superlative not found in PI Section 12.1 text. This claim requires independent
  MLR verification against current FDA approvals as of June 2026.

VEN-SAF-001: BOXED WARNING — Tumor Lysis Syndrome
  Verbatim from PI Section 5.1 and Highlights [Confirmed from PI, Section 5.1]:
  "Tumor lysis syndrome (TLS), including fatal events and renal failure
  requiring dialysis, has occurred in patients treated with VENCLEXTA.
  VENCLEXTA can cause rapid reduction in tumor and thus poses a risk for TLS
  at initiation and during the ramp-up phase in all patients, and during
  reinitiation after dosage interruption in patients with CLL/SLL. Changes in
  blood chemistries consistent with TLS that require prompt management can occur
  as early as 6 to 8 hours following the first dose of VENCLEXTA and at each

  20 mg dose of VENCLEXTA."
  Source: VENCLEXTA PI, Section 5.1 (Warnings and Precautions)

  NOTE [Confirmed from PI]: VENCLEXTA has 1 Boxed Warning (TLS). There is NO

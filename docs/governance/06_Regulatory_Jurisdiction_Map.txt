PURPOSE
-------
This document records the regulatory jurisdictions in scope for PitchMD(tm),
the applicable regulatory framework for each, and the requirements for future
geographic expansion. Each jurisdiction requires its own claim library,
compliance rules, and language localization before deployment.


CURRENT SCOPE — UNITED STATES ONLY (PHASE 1 POC)
--------------------------------------------------
All platform operations in the proof-of-concept phase are governed exclusively
by United States federal regulations.

  REGULATORY BODY  : FDA (Food and Drug Administration)
  KEY FRAMEWORKS   :
    - 21 CFR Part 202: Prescription Drug Advertising
      Governs all promotional claims and fair balance requirements.
    - FDA OPDP (Office of Prescription Drug Promotion) Guidance
      Including guidance on social media, digital communications, and
      interactive media. Key documents:
        * Draft Guidance: Internet/Social Media Platforms (2014)
        * Guidance: Brief Summary and Adequate Directions for Use (2015)
        * Guidance: Presenting Risk Information (2009)
    - PhRMA Code on Interactions with Healthcare Professionals (2022)
      Governs the nature and conduct of rep-HCP communications.
    - 21 CFR Part 11: Electronic Records and Electronic Signatures
      Applies to audit logs and training records. See Document 7.
    - HIPAA Privacy Rule (45 CFR Parts 160 and 164)
      Applies to any incidental processing of PHI in training content.

  US-SPECIFIC IMPLEMENTATION NOTES:
    1. All claims must reference the CURRENT FDA-approved Prescribing
       Information (PI). Prior versions of the PI may not be cited.
    2. Reminder advertisements (name-only, no indication) have different
       fair balance requirements than full product claims. The platform
       must distinguish between these content types.
    3. Per FDA Draft Guidance on Interactive Promotional Media (2014):
       AI-generated responses are treated as promotional communications
       subject to the same standards as traditional materials.
    4. Any mention of an adverse event in a training session — even
       simulated — must be evaluated for MedWatch (FDA Form 3500A)
       reporting obligation. A triage process must be established with
       the Pharmacovigilance team before go-live.
    5. Off-label scientific exchange (MSL activities) is governed by
       different rules than promotional detailing. PitchMD(tm) is scoped
       to promotional training only. MSL mode would require separate
       governance.

SECTION 4.2 — VENCLEXTA-SPECIFIC OFF-LABEL TRIGGERS
------------------------------------------------------
Any mention of the following indications NOT in the Approved Claims Library
triggers an automatic compliance flag and content block:

  OFF-LABEL USES TO BLOCK FOR VENCLEXTA:
  - Multiple Myeloma: VENCLEXTA is NOT approved for multiple myeloma. A
    combination study with bortezomib/dexamethasone showed increased mortality
    in the non-t(11;14) population. Any mention of myeloma use is BLOCKED.
  - Mantle Cell Lymphoma (MCL): VENCLEXTA is not approved for MCL.
  - Follicular Lymphoma (FL): VENCLEXTA is not approved for FL.
  - Pediatric use: VENCLEXTA is approved for adults only; no pediatric
    indication exists.
  - AML as frontline for fit patients: Approved only for adults >=75 years
    old OR with comorbidities that preclude intensive chemotherapy. Use as
    first-line in fit, younger AML patients is off-label.
  - Combination regimens not in the PI: e.g., venetoclax + ibrutinib (CAPTIVATE
    data exists but combination is not FDA-approved as of June 2026 in a single
    label). Confirm current approval status before discussing.
  - Dose modifications outside the PI (e.g., "you can start at 400 mg"
    without the ramp-up in CLL/SLL): BLOCKED — violates Boxed Warning guidance.

  TRIGGER MESSAGE: "I can connect you with our Medical Affairs team for
  information on uses outside the approved indications."


SECTION 4.3 — COMPETITOR DISPARAGEMENT
-----------------------------------------
The following are prohibited. The competitive set includes:
  Imbruvica (ibrutinib), Brukinsa (zanubrutinib), Calquence (acalabrutinib),
  Gazyva (obinutuzumab), Jaypirca (pirtobrutinib), Rituxan (rituximab),
  Copiktra (duvelisib), Zydelig (idelalisib)

  PROHIBITED (Block + Flag):
  - "Imbruvica causes heart attacks" — Disparagement without specific cited
    data. The Section 5.3 Warning and Precaution for cardiac arrhythmias,
    cardiac failure, and sudden death may be cited factually IF quoting
    directly from the approved labeling. NOTE [Confirmed from PI]: IMBRUVICA
    has NO Boxed Warning — do not describe this as a "Boxed Warning."
  - "Brukinsa is just a copy of Calquence" — Unsupported claim;
    different development programs and clinical data.
  - "Copiktra will kill patients" — Inflammatory; the increased mortality
    Boxed Warning must be cited factually from the COPIKTRA PI, not editorialized.
  - "Zydelig was a failure" — Disparaging; the clinical development history
    can be discussed factually.
  - "[Competitor] doesn't work" — Any absolute efficacy dismissal without
    citing specific trial data showing inferiority.
  - "Patients on [competitor] always switch to Venclexta" — Unsupported
    population claim.
  - "You should stop prescribing Ibrutinib immediately" — Direct interference
    with prescriber judgment; not permitted.

  PERMITTED (when citing approved comparative data):
  - Citing ALPINE trial data (zanubrutinib vs. ibrutinib — head-to-head):
    CORRECTION [Confirmed from PI Section 14.4, Table 24, BRUKINSA PI Revised
    6/2025]: The correct ALPINE PFS HR is 0.65 (95% CI 0.49–0.86; p=0.0024),
    NOT "HR 0.68" as previously cited. The HR 0.68 figure is INCORRECT.
    Permitted claim: "The ALPINE trial showed zanubrutinib demonstrated
    superior PFS vs. ibrutinib (HR 0.65; 95% CI 0.49–0.86; p=0.0024)"
    [Confirmed from PI Section 14.4, Table 24, BRUKINSA PI Revised 6/2025]
    Any training material citing "HR 0.68" for ALPINE must be corrected to
    "HR 0.65 (95% CI 0.49–0.86; p=0.0024)".
  - Citing ibrutinib Section 5 Warning content directly:
    "IMBRUVICA carries a Section 5 Warning and Precaution for serious cardiac
    arrhythmias, cardiac failure, and sudden death" — Permitted factual citation
    of a competitor's own PI language.
    CORRECTION [Confirmed from PI, IMBRUVICA PI revised 10/2025]: IMBRUVICA
    has NO Boxed Warnings. Cardiac arrhythmias/cardiac failure/sudden death
    are Section 5.3 Warnings and Precautions, NOT a Boxed Warning. Any training
    material stating "IMBRUVICA carries a Boxed Warning for cardiac arrhythmias"
    is INCORRECT and must be updated. Correct language: "IMBRUVICA Section 5.3
    Warning for Cardiac Arrhythmias, Cardiac Failure, and Sudden Death."
  - Citing ibrutinib Section 5 Warning for Hypertension:
    "IMBRUVICA carries a Section 5.4 Warning for Hypertension" — Permitted if
    citing current PI language exactly.
    CORRECTION [Confirmed from PI, IMBRUVICA PI revised 10/2025]: Hypertension
    is a Section 5.4 Warning and Precaution, NOT a Boxed Warning. The phrase
    "IMBRUVICA carries a Boxed Warning for Hypertension" is PROHIBITED as it
    is factually incorrect per the current PI (10/2025).
  - Citing COPIKTRA Boxed Warning for Treatment-Related Mortality:
    "COPIKTRA carries a Boxed Warning for Treatment-Related Mortality —
    treatment-related deaths occurred in 15% of COPIKTRA-treated patients" —
    Permitted factual citation from current COPIKTRA PI.
    CORRECTION [Confirmed from PI, COPIKTRA PI]: The first Boxed Warning is
    "Treatment-Related Mortality (15% of patients)." The HR 1.09 vs. ofatumumab
    does NOT appear as a Boxed Warning item in the current COPIKTRA PI. The
    increased mortality risk is addressed through the "Treatment-Related
    Mortality" Boxed Warning and the Limitations of Use in Section 1. Any
    training citing "HR 1.09 vs. ofatumumab" as a Boxed Warning item is
    INCORRECT.
  - Citing CLL14 vs. GClb arm (chlorambucil + obinutuzumab) — Permitted
    as this is VENCLEXTA's own trial with an approved comparator.

SECTION 4.4 — TLS SAFETY MINIMIZATION (VENCLEXTA-SPECIFIC)
-------------------------------------------------------------
The following language minimizes the Tumor Lysis Syndrome Boxed Warning
and is PROHIBITED:

  "TLS isn't really a big deal"
  "TLS risk is very rare with proper monitoring"
  "You don't need to worry about TLS for most patients"
  "Just start at the full dose, TLS is manageable"
  "Skip the ramp-up if the patient is low risk"

  ALSO PROHIBITED — RAMP-UP MINIMIZATION:
  "The ramp-up is just a formality"
  "Most physicians skip some ramp-up steps"
  "You can do the ramp-up faster if the patient seems healthy"
  "TLS prophylaxis is optional for most CLL patients"

  COMPLIANT ALTERNATIVE:
  "VENCLEXTA carries a Boxed Warning for TLS, including fatal events and
   renal failure requiring dialysis. The 5-week dose ramp-up schedule
   (20 mg → 50 mg → 100 mg → 200 mg → 400 mg) and prophylaxis measures
   are required for all CLL/SLL patients. With the current ramp-up and
   prophylaxis protocols, the TLS rate was 2% in CLL/SLL monotherapy
   studies."
  NOTE [Confirmed from PI, Section 5.1]: The 2% TLS rate with the current
  5-week ramp-up protocol is confirmed in Section 5.1 of the current VENCLEXTA
  PI (revised 05/2026). The PI also notes that a 13% TLS rate occurred with
  earlier 2-3 week ramp-up protocols. The PI Section 5.1 does specify TLS risk
  stratification by tumor burden (lymph node size and ALC) to guide intensity
  of prophylaxis and monitoring — this stratification language should be
  reviewed by Medical Affairs for potential incorporation into training.

  NOTE: Any training that teaches reps to downplay the TLS Boxed Warning
  creates significant regulatory and liability risk.

  ALSO NOTE — AML TLS RISK:
  The TLS Boxed Warning applies to the AML indication as well. Do not suggest
  that the TLS risk is exclusively a CLL concern. [Confirmed from PI, Section 2.3]:
  The AML ramp-up schedule is shorter than the CLL/SLL 5-week schedule: AML
  patients receive a 3-day ramp-up with azacitidine or decitabine (100 mg Day 1,
  200 mg Day 2, 400 mg Day 3+), and a 4-day ramp-up with low-dose cytarabine
  (100 mg Day 1, 200 mg Day 2, 400 mg Day 3, 600 mg Day 4+). Training must
  accurately reflect these AML-specific dosing schedules per current PI.


SECTION 4.5 — PATIENT IDENTIFICATION AND HIPAA RISK
------------------------------------------------------
Prohibited in all training sessions:

  - Real patient names, dates of birth, medical record numbers
  - Identifiable case descriptions: "My patient at Northwestern last month..."
  - Hospital or practice names combined with patient-specific clinical details
  - Any Protected Health Information (PHI) per 45 CFR Section 164.514
  - Insurance member IDs, claim numbers, payer-specific patient identifiers

  ACTION: Immediate block + Urgent Compliance Flag
  Any PHI detection is escalated to the Compliance Officer.

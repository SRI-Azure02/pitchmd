PURPOSE
-------
This document defines all language, phrases, and claim types that are prohibited
on the PitchMD(tm) platform. These restrictions will be enforced by:

  - Phase 2 (Output Compliance Filter): Scans AI persona responses before
    delivery to the user.
  - Phase 3 (Input Firewall): Scans sales representative inputs before they
    reach the AI model.

Any interaction containing prohibited language is automatically flagged in the
Compliance Audit Log. Blocked content is not delivered to the user.


SECTION 4.1 — ABSOLUTE SUPERLATIVES AND UNSUPPORTED CLAIMS
------------------------------------------------------------
The following terms are prohibited in all platform interactions regardless
of context. Each creates liability for false or misleading promotion.

  PROHIBITED TERM           WHY PROHIBITED
  ------------------------  --------------------------------------------------
  "the best"                Unsupported comparative claim — requires published
                            head-to-head data in approved labeling.

  "the #1 drug"             Unsupported market leadership claim — requires
                            cited, current, in-label comparative data.

  "the most effective"      Unsupported superlative — no universal comparator
                            trial supports an absolute statement.

  "the safest"              No drug is without risk. Absolute safety claims
  "completely safe"         are categorically prohibited by FDA OPDP.
  "zero side effects"

  "proven to cure"          Unapproved cure claim. FDA does not recognize
  "will cure"               "cure" claims except in narrow, specific contexts
                            with supporting clinical evidence.

  "guaranteed"              No therapeutic outcome is guaranteed. Prohibited
  "guaranteed to work"      regardless of what follow-up data is cited.

  "100% effective"          Absolute efficacy claim — unsupported by any
                            clinical trial evidence for any medicine.

  COMPLIANT ALTERNATIVES:
  Use specific, cited language from the Approved Claims Library, e.g.:
    "In the VELORIN-301 trial, Velorin demonstrated a mean HbA1c reduction
     of 1.4% versus 0.7% for placebo (p<0.001)."


SECTION 4.2 — OFF-LABEL INDICATION TRIGGERS
--------------------------------------------
Any mention of the following patterns for products not listed under the
corresponding indication in the Approved Claims Library triggers an automatic
compliance flag and content block:

  - Pediatric or neonatal use for products approved for adults only
  - Combination regimens not described in the approved Prescribing Information
  - Dosing frequencies, routes, or durations outside the PI-specified regimen
  - Disease states, biomarker subpopulations, or lines of therapy not in the
    approved indication statement
  - Use in patient populations explicitly excluded from the approved label
    (e.g., severe renal impairment if contraindicated, pregnancy if Category X)
  - Mentions of investigational or pipeline uses before FDA approval
  - Any phrasing suggesting the product "might work" for an unapproved use

  REDIRECT MESSAGE (displayed to user):
  "I'm only able to discuss FDA-approved indications. For information on
   investigational uses, please contact Medical Affairs."


SECTION 4.3 — COMPETITOR DISPARAGEMENT
----------------------------------------
The following are prohibited in all platform interactions:

  - Direct negative comparisons to competitor products WITHOUT published,
    FDA-recognized head-to-head trial data cited in the approved labeling.
  - Statements implying competitor products are unsafe, ineffective, or
    inferior without specific supporting evidence from approved comparative
    labeling.
  - Language such as:
      "[Competitor] failed"
      "[Competitor] doesn't work"
      "[Competitor] is dangerous"
      "[Competitor] caused [adverse event]" (without cited PI data)
  - Misrepresentation of competitor product labeling or trial methodology.
  - Any disparaging language about competitor company, personnel, or practices.

  NOTE: Comparative claims ARE permitted if and only if:
  (a) the head-to-head data is in the product's approved labeling, AND
  (b) the claim appears in the Approved Claims Library with a valid
      MLR reference number.


SECTION 4.4 — PATIENT IDENTIFICATION AND HIPAA RISK
-----------------------------------------------------
To protect patient privacy and comply with HIPAA (45 CFR Parts 160 and 164),
the following are strictly prohibited in training sessions:

  - Real patient names, dates of birth, medical record numbers, or Social
    Security numbers.
  - Identifiable case descriptions from actual clinical practice, such as:
      "My patient at [Hospital Name] last Tuesday..."
      "I had a 64-year-old woman with [diagnosis] who..."
  - Hospital, clinic, or practice names combined with patient-specific
    clinical details.
  - Any Protected Health Information (PHI) as defined under 45 CFR Section
    164.514.
  - Insurance member IDs, claim numbers, or payer-specific patient identifiers.

  ACTION: Immediate block + Urgent Compliance Flag + user notification.
  Any session containing PHI will be escalated to the Compliance Officer
  regardless of whether the session was completed.


SECTION 4.5 — FINANCIAL AND LEGAL TOPICS
-----------------------------------------
The following topics are outside the promotional domain and must be redirected:

  - Drug pricing, net price, or list price discussions
  - Rebate structures, contract terms, or discount negotiations
  - Insurance coverage guarantees or payer access promises
  - Legal advice or regulatory interpretation
  - Investment, pipeline, or financial performance information

  REDIRECT MESSAGE:
  "Pricing and contract discussions are handled by your account manager.
   Let's refocus on the clinical profile."
